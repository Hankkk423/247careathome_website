FAQ for our Home Health Services

Home Health care provides medical services at home for individuals with acute or chronic conditions.

Home Health care is recommended when you require medical care but prefer to receive it in the comfort of your home.

Qualification depends on your medical condition and the specific criteria of your insurance plan.

Home Health services include skilled nursing, physical therapy, occupational therapy, and more, tailored to your needs.

Home Health care is typically covered by Medicare, Medicaid, and many private insurance plans.

In most cases, you can choose your Home Health provider, ensuring you receive the care you prefer.

The frequency of visits depends on your condition, with the team working closely with your healthcare provider.

Yes, you can maintain your relationship with your primary care physicians while receiving Home Health care.

During a visit, a nurse or therapist will provide medical care, assess your progress, and answer any questions.

To begin Home Health care, consult with your healthcare provider or contact a local Home Health agency for an assessment.
